Emilia-Romagna, Italia

Travel Guide

Everything you need to explore one of Italy's most beautiful regions

Getting There

Flying to Italy

Bologna is our home base, and there are several airports that can get you there. A heads-up: non-direct flights through Europe often come with delays — if you're connecting, aim for a layover of at least 2.5 hours to be safe.

Bologna

BLQ

Fly direct into Bologna. The easiest and most convenient option — no train needed.

Florence

FLR

A 40–60 minute train ride to Bologna. Smaller airport with manageable crowds.

Venice

VCE — Marco Polo

Smallest of the major airports with the shortest wait times. About one hour to Bologna by train.

Milan

MXP — Malpensa

Less congested than Rome. Approximately 3 hours to Bologna by train.

Rome

FCO — Fiumicino

Many direct flight options from across the US. About 3 hours to Bologna by train.

Getting to the Hotel

The Amati Design Hotel offers complimentary transportation from both the airport and the train station. Contact them directly to arrange pickup: +39 051 758093.

Food & Drink

Eat Your Way Through Emilia-Romagna

This region is considered the food capital of Italy — home of Bolognese, Parmigiano-Reggiano, Prosciutto di Parma, and fresh handmade pasta. Come hungry.

Must-Try Pasta

  • Tortellini in cream sauce
  • Tagliatelle al ragù
  • Lasagna verde (the original)
  • Tortelloni with ricotta

Meat & Charcuterie

  • Prosciutto di Parma
  • Mortadella di Bologna
  • Salami
  • Cotoletta Bolognese (veal cutlet)
  • Coniglio alla cacciatora (rabbit cacciatore)

Bakeries & Sweets

  • Pistachio doughnuts
  • Cream-filled pastries
  • Café cornetto e cappuccino

Start every morning at a local bar (café) — it's the Italian way.

Gelato

  • Gelato Fini
  • La Vecchia Stalla
  • Cremeria Cavour

Look for "artigianale" signs — that means it's made fresh on-site.

Restaurant Pick

  • Trattoria Giampi e Ciccio

A local favorite — make a reservation in advance.

Grocery Finds

  • Fonzies chips
  • Piadina bread
  • Local cookies & beer

A Conad or Coop grocery store is a great place to stock up on snacks for day trips.

Bologna

Things to See & Do

Le Due Torri

The iconic medieval towers at the heart of Bologna. Climb the taller Asinelli tower for panoramic views of the city.

Santuario di San Luca

A stunning basilica perched on the hills above Bologna, connected to the city by the world's longest portico — a beautiful walk.

San Pietro Cathedral

Bologna's main cathedral, dating to the 10th century. Worth a visit for the art and architecture inside.

Santo Stefano Complex

A fascinating collection of seven interconnected churches that date back to the 5th century. One of Bologna's hidden gems.

Giardini Margherita

Bologna's largest public park — perfect for a leisurely morning walk or afternoon picnic.

Shopping

Piazza Cavour for luxury boutiques. Zara and Brandy Melville for everyday fashion. Kiko for Italian cosmetics.

While You're Here

Day Trips from Bologna

Bologna is perfectly positioned for exploring northern Italy. All of the following cities are easily reachable by regional train.

Florence · ~40 min Venice · ~2 hrs Verona · ~1.5 hrs Parma · ~1 hr Modena · ~30 min
